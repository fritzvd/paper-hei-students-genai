Introduction

Recent developments have seen generative Artificial Intelligence (genAI) being available for the masses with Large Language Models (LLM’s) having a much easier interface for non-technical users. Often signified with the moniker: AI or with magical stars (✨) it features in many applications ranging from web search to (Google n.d.; DuckDuckGo n.d.; Microsoft n.d.) personal communication such as Outlook (“Chat with Copilot in Outlook - Microsoft Support” n.d.) or Whatsapp (Meta n.d.). genAI is quickly becoming embedded in all sorts of business and applications (Grantham-Philips 2024). Even more than in applications beforehand users are encouraged to upload documents and provide more context for the LLM to perform better.

As a result of the increased use of genAI, such as LLM’s, a great variety of topics have been researched in the last few years. Such as environmental impact (Ding and Shi 2024; Rillig et al. 2023), closing and / or widening of the digital divide (Carter, Liu, and Cantrell 2020; Sartori and Bocca 2023). In the context of education, and higher education specifically research has been conducted concerning a number of topics including: guidelines and policy (Wang et al. 2024), about usage, knowledge and skills (Suonpää, Heikkilä, and Dimkar 2024; Silvennoinen, Aksovaara, and Alanko-Turunen 2025), personalized learning (Zhang, Yao, and Hadizadeh Moghaddam 2025; Udeh 2025), the effect on learning outcomes (Han, Peng, and Liu 2025) and the use of genAI in collaborative learning (Perifanou and Economides 2025).
